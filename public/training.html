How to Recognize Phishing Attempts

  • Check the sender: Verify the email address carefully. Phishers often use addresses that look similar to legitimate ones.
  • Look for urgency: Be suspicious of emails that create a sense of urgency or threaten consequences.
  • Hover before clicking: Hover your mouse over links to see the actual URL before clicking.
  • Watch for poor grammar: Many phishing emails contain spelling and grammar mistakes.
  • Verify requests: If an email asks for sensitive information, contact the sender through a known, trusted channel.
  • Be cautious with attachments: Don't open unexpected attachments, even if they appear to be from someone you know.

What to Do If You Suspect Phishing

  • Do not click any links or download attachments
  • Report the email to your IT security team immediately
  • Delete the email after reporting it
  • If you've already clicked a link, report it to IT security right away

Remember: Security is everyone's responsibility. Stay vigilant and think before you click!
